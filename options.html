PIT Abuse Reporter – Settings

How the addon finds the correct abuse address

When reporting unsolicited email, it is important to send the report to the correct abuse contact of the sender's domain. Many domains publish this information using modern email security standards.

About DMARC

DMARC is a technology used to specify how email from a domain should be authenticated. One of its functions is to publish an official reporting address where email issues or possible abuse can be reported.

This address is stored in a special DNS record named _dmarc.example.com. A typical DMARC record may contain: 

v=DMARC1; p=none; rua=mailto:abuse@example.com

The addon can read this record and automatically use the reporting address declared by the domain owner.

Fallback: generic abuse@domain

Some domains do not publish a DMARC reporting address. In this case, the addon will use the traditional fallback: 

abuse@example.com

If you prefer, you may disable DMARC detection entirely and always use this generic address.
